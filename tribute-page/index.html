Dr. Jane Goodall

A Life Dedicated to Understanding and Conserving Wildlife

Here's a timeline of Dr. Goodall's life:

  • 1934 - Born in London, England, developing a passion for animals and Africa from an early age.
  • 1957 - Travels to Kenya, where she meets renowned anthropologist Louis Leakey, who would become her mentor.
  • 1960 - Begins her field study in Gombe Stream National Park, Tanzania, where she makes groundbreaking discoveries, such as chimpanzees using tools—a behavior previously thought to be unique to humans.
  • 1965 - Earns her Ph.D. in Ethology from the University of Cambridge, one of the few people to achieve this without a prior bachelor’s degree.
  • 1977 - Founds the Jane Goodall Institute, an organization dedicated to wildlife research, education, and conservation.
  • 1986 - Publishes her book The Chimpanzees of GombeThe Chimpanzees of Gombe, summarizing over 25 years of research and raising awareness about habitat destruction and animal welfare.
  • 1991 - Establishes Roots & Shoots, a global youth-led program focused on environmental and humanitarian issues.
  • 2002 - Named a United Nations Messenger of Peace, reflecting her advocacy for environmental protection and animal rights on an international scale.
  • 2020 - Continues her mission through virtual talks, sharing her message of hope and conservation with audiences worldwide.
  • Present - Dr. Goodall remains a leading voice in environmental conservation and an inspiration to scientists and activists globally.
